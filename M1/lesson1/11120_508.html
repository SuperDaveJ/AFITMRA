Purpose

The purpose of the Engineering and Manufacturing Development & Demonstration phase is to develop a system or an increment of capability; complete full system integration; develop an affordable and executable manufacturing process; ensure operational supportability with particular attention to minimizing the logistics footprint; implement human systems integration (HSI); design for producibility; ensure affordability; protect Critical Program Information (CPI) by implementing appropriate techniques such as anti-tamper; and demonstrate system integration, interoperability, safety, and utility.

Definition

Manufacturing capability is in place to begin LRIP.

Description

The design is sufficiently stable to enter into LRIP. Physical and functional interfaces are clearly defined. All materials are in production and available to meet the planned LRIP schedule. Manufacturing and quality processes and procedures have been proven, are under control, and are ready for LRIP. Initial producibility risk assessments have been completed. Production cost estimates meet DTC goals. The supply chain has been established and is stable. Key technologies are at TRL 8 at the least.

Key Manufacturing Considerations

  • The Industrial Capability Assessment (ICA) for milestone C has been completed. Industrial capability is in place to support LRIP.Â 
  • Required manufacturing technology solutions have been validated on a pilot line.
  • Known producibility issues have been resolved and pose no significant risk for LRIP.
  • Detailed design of product features and interfaces is complete.
  • Major product design features are sufficiently stable such that key LRIP manufacturing processes will be representative of those used in FRP.
  • Engineering cost model is driven by detailed design and validated with data from a relevant environment.
  • A cost analysis of proposed changes to requirements or configuration is complete.
  • Yields and Rates have been evaluated in a production relevant environment.
  • Materials have been proven and validated on SDD production as adequate to support LRIP.
  • Long Lead procurement has been initiated for LRIP. Availability issues pose no significant risk for LRIP.
  • Most material decisions have been made (make / buy), material risk has been identified, and plans have been made to mitigate risks.
  • Prototype tooling concepts have been demonstrated in a relevant manufacturing environment.

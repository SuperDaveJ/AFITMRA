Milestone B Relationships

Purpose
The purpose of the Technology Development phase is to reduce technology risk and to determine the appropriate set of technologies to be integrated into a full system.
Definition
Manufacturing capability is in place to produce the system, component, or item in a production-representative environment.
Description
Majority of manufacturing requirements have been preliminarily defined and validated, to include key characteristics, but there are still significant engineering / design changes. Preliminary design of critical components has been completed. Component physical and functional interfaces have not yet been defined. Materials, machines and tooling, personnel skills, and Inspection and test equipment have been demonstrated in a relevant environment but most manufacturing processes and procedures are in development (or MANTECH initiatives are ongoing). Producibility assessments are ongoing. Production cost drivers / goals have been analyzed. System level Design-to-Cost (DTC) goals are set. Long lead needs and key supply chain elements have been identified.
Supporting Information
  • Industrial capability is in place to support manufacturing of development articles.
  • Required manufacturing technology development solutions have been demonstrated in a production relevant environment.
  • Producibility assessments of key technologies / components and producibility trade studies have been completed.
  • Key Characteristics and tolerances have been established.
  • Lead times have been identified for all materials.
  • Cost model inputs include design requirements, material specifications, tolerances, integrated master schedule, results of system / subsystem simulations, and production relevant demonstrations.
  • Material maturity has been verified through technology demonstration articles.
  • Availability issues have been addressed to meet technology demonstration articles.
  • Supply chain plans are in place.
  • Plans to address special handling requirements are complete
  • Initial simulation models have been developed at the technology, sub-system, or system level.
  • Manufacturing processes have been demonstrated in a production relevant environment.
  • Yields and Rates have been evaluated in a production relevant environment.
  • An initial Quality Plan and Quality Management System are in place.
  • Manufacturing workforce skills are available for production in a relevant environment.
  • Manufacturing facility and facility development plans are adequate to support System Development and Demonstration (SDD) or Technology insertion.
  • Manufacturing risk mitigation approach for SDD or Technology insertion Programs has been defined.
  • Most material decisions have been made (make / buy), material risk has been identified, and plans made to mitigate risk.
  • Prototype tooling concepts have been demonstrated in a relevant manufacturing environment.

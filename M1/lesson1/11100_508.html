Purpose

The purpose of the MSA phase is to refine the initial concept chosen and develop a Technology Development Strategy (TDS) that will guide the execution of the Technology Development Phase.  

Definition

Manufacturing capability is in place to produce the system, component, or item in a laboratory environment.

Description

Conceptual design has been completed. Requirement validation is underway and there are large numbers of engineering / design changes. Required investments, such as MANTECH, have been identified. Component physical and functional interfaces have not been defined. Processes to insure producibility, manufacturability, and quality are in place and are sufficient to produce technology demonstrators.  Manufacturing cost drivers are emerging. Producibility assessments have been initiated. Key technologies are at TRL 4 at the least.

Key Manufacturing Considerations

  • Industrial Base capabilities have been identified for key technologies and key processes.
  • Producibility & Manufacturability assessment of design concepts are complete. 
  • Establishment / validation of manufacturing capability and management of manufacturing risk for the product lifecycle are complete. 
  • Initial Key Performance Parameters (KPPs) have been identified.
  • Producibility cost risks have been assessed.
  • A survey has been completed to determine if materials have been used before.
  • Lead times have been identified for all materials.
  • A survey has been completed for potential supply chain sources.
  • Special handling requirements have been identified.
  • A survey has been completed to determine the current state of proposed processes.
  • Yield and Rates have been assessed on proposed processes.
  • Quality strategy has been developed.
  • Manufacturing skill sets have been identified.
  • Specialized facility requirements / needs have been identified.
  • Special Tooling / Special Test Equipment (STE) requirements are considered.

Now that you know what a Manufacturing Readiness Assessment is, let's move into a discussion about why we perform it. What purpose does it serve? Let’s look at that now.

The purpose of a Manufacturing Readiness Assessment is to evaluate a programs’ ability to produce on-schedule while meeting cost and performance requirements and to develop and implement manufacturing risk mitigation plans and business strategies. This is important because immature manufacturing development often leads to cost overruns, schedule slippages, and performance problems.

To understand this purpose, consider the 2009 Government Accountability Office Report to Congressional Committees of 47 Department of Defense programs. This study was conducted to show the effects of moving forward in a program without having requisite knowledge at key decision points. 

The Government Accountability Office followed those programs over a seven year period. Very few of them used statistical process control data to measure the maturity of their production processes. The study found a cost increase of 42%, with an estimated total acquisition cost growth of $296 billion for those 47 programs over 7 years. In addition, it found that the average schedule delay was 22 months. The conclusion of the analysis was that that one of the three key contributors to the cost and schedule growth was not adequately understanding and addressing Manufacturing / Quality issues during development and production.

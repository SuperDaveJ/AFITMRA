What are the roles and responsibilities of the individuals in the MRA process?

SAF / AQR

The SAF / AQR is also known as the Component S & T Executive.

The SAF / AQR serves as AF lead for MRA policy, guidance, and oversight. The SAF / AQR also directs and endorses the MRAs required for acquisition programs.

PM at the PMO

An individual designated by DoDD 5000.01 who has the authority to accomplish program objectives for development, productions and sustainment to meet user’s operational needs. The PM has total lifecycle system management authority.

The PM is directly responsible for the MRA process, as the process directly relates to the a comprehensive integrated risk analysis for the acquisition program. The PM also identifies members of the MRA Team and designates the Team Lead.

Aeronautical Centers

Each Aeronautical Center has specific policies in place regarding mandatory MRA activities.

Currently, AACs require MRAs prior to Milestones B and C and prior to a FRP decision.

ASCs do not currently share this requirement, but a policy letter is in the works to do so.

Team Lead

The Team Lead is responsible for the actions of the MRA Team Members and the administration of the MRA process. Specific activities for the Team Lead include:

  • Evaluating and adjusting the MRA scope, as necessary
  • Scheduling on-site assessments with the contractor(s)
  • Sending the orientation package to the contractor(s)
  • Defining the deliverables of the assessment
  • Conducting the on-site assessment with the contractor(s)
  • Delivering the final report / briefing

MRA Team Members

The MRA Team is identified to perform an MRA. The team composition will normally lean heavily toward program office and service manufacturing SMEs.

Which policies and directives affect a MRA?

  • AFPD 63-1
  • AFI 63-101
  • AAC Policies
  • ASC Policies

What are the MRA activities that are appropriate for each phase of the acquisition lifecycle?

Capabilities Based Planning / Concept Development

MRL 1

Basic manufacturing implications identified

MRL 2

Manufacturing concepts identified

MRL 3

Manufacturing proof of concept developed

Materiel Solution Analysis

MRL 4

Capability to produce the technology in a laboratory environment

Technology Development

MRL 5

Capability to produce prototype components in a production relevant environment

MRL 6

Capability to produce a prototype system or subsystem in a production relevant environment

Engineering & Manufacturing Development

MRL 7

Capability to produce systems, subsystems, or components in a production representative environment

MRL 8

Pilot line capability demonstrated; ready to begin LRIP

Production & Deployment / Operations & Support

MRL 9

LRIP demonstrated; capability in place to begin FRP

MRL 10

FRP demonstrated and lean production practices in place

What are the ways that MRA can be integrated into contract language?

The suggested best practices for MRA incorporation into contract language include:

  • Place MRLs and MRAs into the contract at the start of the program
  • Define the MRLs needed to execute the program
  • Require the contractor to perform assessments
  • Monitor the performance of assessments on a continuing basis
  • Connect MRL / MRA activity to formal reviews
  • Incorporate MRAs into existing contracts
  • Specify the right deliverables

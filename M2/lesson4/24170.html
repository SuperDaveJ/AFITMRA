MRL Threads

MRLs have been organized into 9 subject areas:

  • Technology and Industrial Base
  • Design
  • Cost and Funding
  • Materials
  • Process Capability and Control
  • Quality Management
  • Manufacturing Personnel
  • Facilities
  • Manufacturing Management

Technology and the Industrial Base

  • TRLs are assessed
  • Manufacturing technology is engaged
  • Industrial Base is assessed
  • Industrial Base capabilities / risks are identified
  • Industrial Capability Assessment for MS B & C is completed
  • Sole / single / foreign source stability is assessed
  • Required manufacturing technology is developed / identified / demonstrated / validated

 

Design

  • Systems Engineering Plan is validated for manufacturing processes
  • Producibility & manufacturability assessments are completed
  • Producibility enhancement efforts are implemented
  • Key Characteristics are identified and risks addressed
  • The percentage drawings complete is tracked
  • Key Performance Parameters are identified

 

Materials

  • Supply chain sources / risks are identified Diminishing Manufacturing Sources and Material Shortages is identified
  • Obsolescence plan is in place
  • Sole / single / foreign sources are identified
  • Long-lead items are identified
  • Special handling requirements are identified
  • Supply chain management process is effective
  • Government Furnished Equipment / Property (GFE / GFP) is properly managed

 

 

Cost and Funding

  • Cost models are developed
  • Cost in relation to Manufacturing interactions & flow of product is identified
  • Program budgets include manufacturing costs for Risk Mitigation Plans
  • Capital investment is identified
  • Budget estimating is completed
  • Value Stream Map cost model is completed
  • Costs / targets for system components are identified
  • Costs for Make / Buy are identified

 

Process Capability and Control

  • Modeling & simulation is used
  • Process yield & rate issues are identified
  • Process capability is demonstrated - Stable process vs. unstable process
  • 3-sigma / 6-sigma capability is demonstrated
  • Critical processes are identified
  • Variation of processes is identified and addressed
  • Statistical Process Capability is implemented
  • Continuous process improvement is implemented
  • Lean philosophy is implemented

 

Quality Management

  • Quality strategy is refined in Systems Engineering Plan
  • Program / contractor Quality Plan and / or Manual is in place
  • Quality risks and metrics are identified
  • Advanced Quality Management System is in place
  • Demonstrated ability to collect & analyze quality data
  • Continuous quality improvement is implemented
  • Key Characteristics are identified

 

Manufacturing Personnel

  • New manufacturing skills are identified
  • Special skills certification & training is identified / implemented
  • Manufacturing workforce resource requirements are identified
  • Lean & Six-Sigma training is implemented

 

Facilities

  • Specialized facility needs are identified
  • Industrial Base capability is identified for key technologies â€“ gaps are identified
  • Manufacturing facilities and facility development is identified to support Low Rate Initial Production & Full Rate Production
  • Plant layout and design is initiated / implemented
  • Capital equipment is identified / installed
  • Tooling is identified / installed

 

Manufacturing Management

  • Tooling / Special Test Equipment / Special Inspection Equipment is identified / installed
  • Manufacturing processes and procedures are established / controlled to 6-sigma level
  • Manufacturing risks are identified and integrated into the Risk Mitigation Plan
  • Bill of Material is initiated / completed
  • Work Instructions are completed / verified
  • Make / Buy decisions are implemented
  • Manufacturing interactions and flow of product is identified
  • Continuous process improvement and Lean philosophy is implemented
  • Material Requirements Planning (MRP) / Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) / Systems, Applications and Products (SAP) implemented
  • Integrated Master Plan (IMP) / Integrated Master Schedule (IMS) addresses manufacturing events

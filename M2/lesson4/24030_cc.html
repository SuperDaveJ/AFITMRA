Ms. Sherrill Buchanan

Previous experience from the field experts has revealed four best practices.

First: Assign specific technologies, assemblies, subsystems, or processes to individuals or sub teams. This is especially helpful when working with larger assessments.

Second: Inform the contractor in advance if there are high-interest areas where shop floor visits and / or discussions with contractor experts will be requested.

Third: Seek out documentation that supports the contractor self-assessment results. For instance, any plans, yield data, reports, briefings, work instructions, etc.

And lastly: If there is any information the contractor was unable to provide in a key area, assign an action item to provide that information by a specific date.

Ms. Janet Wood

When I sent the orientation package to the contractor, I informed them that we would need to visit the shop floor.  After I received their self assessment back, myself and the team gathered supporting documentation to correlate the results. Finally, I decided that I’d break the team into three groups of two to cover more ground in a timely manner during the assessment.

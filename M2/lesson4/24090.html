MRLs Revisited

Production Relevant Environment

This environment is normally found during MRL 5 and 6. It contains the key elements of production realism (production personnel, materials or equipment or tooling, process steps, work instructions, stated cycle time, etc.) that are not normally found in the laboratory environment. This environment may occur in a laboratory or in a model shop, providing that key elements or production realism is added.

Production Representative Environment

This environment is normally found during MRL 7, probably on the manufacturing floor. It contains most of the key elements (tooling, equipment, temperature, cleanliness, lighting, personnel skill levels, materials, work instructions, etc.) that will be present on the shop floor production areas when low rate production takes place.

Pilot Line Environment

This environment is normally found during MRL 8 in a manufacturing floor production area that incorporates all of the key elements (equipment, personnel skill levels, materials, components, work instructions, tooling, etc.) required to produce production configuration items, subsystems, or systems that meet design requirements in low rate production. To the maximum extent practical, the pilot line should utilize rate production processes.
